The Planets in our Solar System

    Here is a detailed look at the eight planets in our Solar System, including quick facts and data tables.

  • Mercury

    Mercury is the closest planet to our Sun, at just 58 million km out. It is also the smallest planet in our Solar System.

    Diameter Mass Orbit Weight (relative)
    4,879 km 3.3011 x 10^23 kg 87.97 Earth days 38% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Mercury

  • Venus

    Venus is Earth's "sister planet" and the hottest planet in the Solar System due to its thick atmosphere.

    Diameter Temperature Day Length Weight (relative)
    12,104 km 462° C 243 Earth days 91% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Venus

  • Earth

    Our home, the only planet known to support life, used as the benchmark for all planetary metrics.

    Diameter Mass Moons Atmosphere
    12,760 km 5.97 x 10^24 kg 1 Moon 78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen
    Quick Facts: Earth

  • Mars

    The "Red Planet", known for its iron-oxide surface and similarities to Earth's rotational patterns.

    Diameter Temperature Moons Weight (relative)
    6,787 km Average -55° C Phobos, Deimos 38% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Mars

  • Jupiter

    The gas giant, most massive planet in our system, 317 times more massive than Earth.

    Diameter Orbit Moons Weight (relative)
    142,800 km 11.9 Earth years 79 Moons 253% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Jupiter

  • Saturn

    Recognized by its spectacular ring system made of ice and rock particles.

    Diameter Temperature Moons Weight (relative)
    120,500 km -178° C 62 Moons 107% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Saturn

  • Uranus

    An "Ice Giant" with methane ice that gives it a distinct bluish hue.

    Diameter Orbit Moons Weight (relative)
    51,120 km 84 Earth years 27 Moons 91% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Uranus

  • Neptune

    The farthest planet, also an Ice Giant with extreme wind speeds and high methane content.

    Diameter Orbit Moons Weight (relative)
    49,530 km 165 Earth years 14 Moons 114% of Earth weight
    Quick Facts: Neptune
