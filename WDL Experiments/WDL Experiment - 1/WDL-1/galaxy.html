The Planets in our Solar System

    Having covered the basics of definition and classification, let’s get talking about those celestial bodies in our Solar System that are still classified as planets (sorry Pluto!). Here is a brief look at the eight planets in our Solar System. Included are quick facts and links so you can find out more about each planet.

  • Mercury

    Mercury is the closest planet to our Sun, at just 58 million km (36 million miles) or 0.39 Astronomical Unit (AU) out. But despite its reputation for being sun-baked and molten, it is not the hottest planet in our Solar System.

    Mercury is also the smallest planet in our Solar System, and is also smaller than its largest moon (Ganymede, which orbits Jupiter). And being equivalent in size to 0.38 Earths, it is just slightly larger than the Earth’s own Moon.

    Mercury Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    4,879 km (3,032 miles) 3.3011 x 10^23 kg (0.055 Earths) 87.97 Earth days 59 Earth days None -173 to 427 °C 45 kg on Earth = 17 kg on Mercury

  • Venus

    Venus is the second closest planet to our Sun. It is often called Earth’s “sister planet,” as it is just a little smaller than Earth. Due to its thick atmosphere and proximity to the Sun, it is the Solar Systems hottest planet, with temperatures reaching up to a scorching 735 K (462 °C).

    Venus Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    12,104 km (7,521 miles) 4.867 x 10^24 kg (0.815 Earths) 225 days 243 Earth days None 462 °C (864 °F) 45 kg on Earth = 41 kg on Venus

  • Earth

    Our home, and the only planet in our Solar System (that we know of) that actively supports life. It is the third planet from our Sun, orbiting at an average distance of 150 million km (93 million miles).

    Earth Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Atmosphere
    12,760 km (7,926 miles) 5.97 x 10^24 kg 365 days 24 hours One Moon Avg. 14 °C (-88 to 58) 78% N2, 21% O2

  • Mars

    Mars is the fourth planet from the sun at a distance of about 228 million km (142 million miles). It is also known as “the Red Planet” because of its reddish hue, due to the prevalence of iron oxide on its surface.

    Mars Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    6,787 km (4,217 miles) 6.4171 x 10^23 kg (0.107 Earths) 687 Earth days 24 hours 37 minutes 2 (Phobos, Deimos) Avg. -55 °C 45 kg on Earth = 17 kg on Mars

  • Jupiter

    Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun, and the most massive planet in our Solar System, being 317 times the mass of Earth. It is a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.

    Jupiter Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    142,800 km (88,730 miles) 1.8986 × 10^27 kg (317.8 Earths) 11.9 Earth years 9.8 Earth hours 79 identified moons -148 °C (-234 °F) 45 kg on Earth = 115 kg on Jupiter

  • Saturn

    Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and a gas giant like Jupiter. It is most famous and easily recognized for its spectacular ring system, which is made of seven rings composed of ice and rock particles.

    Saturn Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    120,500 km (74,900 miles) 5.6836 x 10^26 kg (95.159 Earths) 29.5 Earth years 10.7 Earth hours 53 known (+9 pending) -178 °C (-288 °F) 45 kg on Earth = 48 kg on Saturn

  • Uranus

    Uranus is the seventh planet from the sun and is often referred to as an “ice giant” due to the presence of ammonia, methane, water, and hydrocarbons in ice form. Its bluish appearance comes from methane ice.

    Uranus Quick Facts
    Diameter Mass Length of Orbit Length of Day Moons Temperature Weight
    51,120 km (31,763 miles) 8.681 × 10^25 kg (14.5 Earths) 84 Earth years 18 Earth hours 27 moons -216 °C (-357 °F) 45 kg on Earth = 41 kg on Uranus

  • Neptune

    Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It is similar in many respects to Uranus, consisting of gases, ices, and methane ice, with a series of moons and faint rings.

    Neptune Quick Facts

    49,530 km (30,775 miles) 1.0243 x 10^26 kg (17 Earths) 165 Earth years 16 Earth hours 13 confirmed (+1 pending) -214 °C (-353 °F) 45 kg on Earth = 52 kg on Neptune
